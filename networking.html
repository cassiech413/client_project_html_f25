Networking & Connections Tips

Introduction of Making Connections With People

Building a professional network is more than asking for jobs—it's about creating authentic relationships, learning from others, and discovering opportunities you might not find online. Start small: talk to classmates, join student organizations, and attend events. These small steps can open doors to meaningful long-term connections.

Steps to Building Connections

  1. Find Alumni: Use UMSI resources such as the LinkedIn Group, Alumni Tool, CareerLink, UCAN, or attend networking/recruiting events. Don't forget your own network—friends, peers, faculty, mentors, or LinkedIn searches.
  2. Get a Response: Don't get discouraged by low response rates—20% is normal. Aim to move the conversation forward through a phone call, video chat, coffee meeting, or email exchange.
    • Do: Keep it short, set a clear time frame (e.g., 20 minutes), personalize your message.
    • Don't: Ask for info you can Google, send your resume right away, or immediately request a job/referral.

Building Connections Through TIARA Method

The TIARA method gives you a simple framework to guide your conversations, helping you build meaningful connections while learning from professionals.

  • T - Trends: What trends do you see shaping this field?
  • I - Insights: What do you enjoy most about your current role?
  • A - Advice: What advice would you give to students entering this industry?
  • R - Resources: Are there resources you recommend (books, communities, courses)?
  • A - Assignments: What's one thing I should try doing in the next 30 days?

Networking FAQ

  • Do I need to network if I'm not looking for a job right now?
    Yes! Networking is about building long-term relationships. Starting early makes it easier to reach out when you do need advice or opportunities.
  • What if I feel shy or don't know what to say?
    You don't need to have all the answers—just be curious. Ask people about their career path, challenges, and advice. Most professionals enjoy sharing their story.
  • How many informational interviews should I aim for?
    There's no magic number, but a good goal is 2-3 per month. Focus on quality conversations rather than quantity.
  • Is it okay to reach out to someone I don't know on LinkedIn?
    Yes, as long as your message is polite, personalized, and concise. Mention something specific you admire about their work to increase your chances of getting a response.
  • What should I do after a networking conversation?
    Always send a thank-you note or email within 24 hours. Stay in touch by updating them on your progress or sharing resources they might find useful.
